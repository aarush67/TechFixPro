Fix Overheating on Windows

Overheating can damage your system. Follow these steps to prevent overheating.

Back to Blogs

1. Clean Your PC's Vents

Dust buildup can block airflow, causing overheating:

Step 1: Turn off your PC and unplug it from the power source.

Step 2: Use compressed air to blow out dust from the vents and fans.

2. Check for Malware

Malware can use up system resources, causing excess heat:

Step 1: Run a full malware scan using Windows Security or a third-party antivirus tool.

Step 2: Remove any threats found during the scan.

3. Ensure Proper Ventilation

Ensure your PC is in a well-ventilated area to help cool it down:

Step 1: Place your PC in a room with good air circulation.

Step 2: Avoid placing your PC on soft surfaces like beds or couches.

By following these steps, you can prevent your PC from overheating and improve its longevity.
